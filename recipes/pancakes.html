Pancakes

Pancakes are a simple and comforting dish, popular for breakfast or brunch.

The recipe starts with a smooth batter made from flour, milk, eggs, and a touch of sugar, mixed until just combined to keep the texture light. A little baking powder is added to help the pancakes rise and become fluffy. The batter is then poured in small portions onto a heated pan or griddle, lightly greased, and cooked until bubbles form on the surface before being flipped to achieve a golden colour on both sides. Once cooked, pancakes are best served warm, stacked and topped with a variety of ingredients such as maple syrup, fresh fruit, butter, or chocolate. Their soft and airy texture pairs well with both sweet and savoury flavours, making them highly versatile.

Whether prepared for a quick breakfast or a relaxed weekend meal, pancakes are appreciated for their simplicity, quick preparation, and satisfying taste.

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup all-purpose flour

  • 2 tablespoons sugar

  • 1 tablespoon baking powder

  • 1/2 teaspoon salt

  • 1 cup milk

  • 1 large egg

  • 2 tablespoons melted butter or vegetable oil

Instructions:

  1. In a large bowl, whisk together the flour, sugar, baking powder, and salt.

  2. In another bowl, beat the milk and egg until well combined. Add the melted butter or oil and mix.

  3. Pour the wet ingredients into the dry ingredients and stir until just combined. Do not overmix.

  4. Heat a lightly oiled griddle or pan over medium heat. Pour batter onto the griddle in small portions.

  5. Cook until bubbles form on the surface, then flip and cook until golden brown on the other side.

  6. Serve warm with your choice of toppings. Enjoy!
